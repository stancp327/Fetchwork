## 4. How Payment Protection Works

FetchWork uses a **payment credit system** to protect both clients and freelancers.

### How it works:
1. **Client funds a job** — When a client hires a freelancer, payment is collected and held as **platform credits** on the client's behalf.
2. **Freelancer completes the work** — The freelancer delivers the agreed-upon work.
3. **Client approves** — Once the client confirms satisfactory completion, the platform credits are released to the freelancer's account balance.
4. **Freelancer withdraws** — Freelancers can withdraw their available balance to their connected bank account or debit card via Stripe.

### Important:
- Platform credits are **not** a stored-value account, deposit account, or e-money. They represent a contractual obligation by FetchWork to facilitate payment between parties upon work completion.
- Credits cannot be transferred between users except through completed transactions on the platform.
- FetchWork reserves the right to hold credits during an active dispute resolution process.

## 8. Refunds & Cancellations

### Before Work Begins
- Clients may cancel a job before a freelancer is hired for a full refund of any platform credits.
- After a freelancer is hired but before work begins, cancellation terms depend on the agreed cancellation policy (Flexible, Moderate, or Strict) selected for the job or service.

### Cancellation Tiers
- **Flexible:** Full refund if cancelled 24+ hours before the scheduled start. 50% refund for 2–24 hours. No refund under 2 hours.
- **Moderate:** Full refund if cancelled 48+ hours before. 50% refund for 24–48 hours. No refund under 24 hours.
- **Strict:** Full refund if cancelled 7+ days before. 50% refund for 48 hours–7 days. No refund under 48 hours.

### Disputes
If work quality is unsatisfactory, clients may open a dispute before releasing payment. See Section 9.

### No Refunds After Release
Once a client approves and releases payment credits to a freelancer, the transaction is final and non-refundable through FetchWork.
